Minimal Players Needed -- Immaculate Grid Solutions



INTRODUCTION

In 2023, discusssions on Reddit /baseball and the Facebook "because it's baseball" group asked what the minimum number of players needed to be able to fill every possible square on an Immaculate Grid where the row and column were both MLB teams. The concensus then was that you needed 19 players to do this.

Fast forward to 2026. Three years later, more players have played on more teams and it now appears possible that only 18 players are needed. This page contains lists of groups of players that are 18 players long, and for each list a tedious table of team pairs demonstrating that at least one player from the group has played on both those teams.

CAVEATS

Teams have moved and changed names over the years. Because of this, the following teams are considered to be the same team:

  • Angels -- Los Angeles Angels, Anaheim Angels, California Angels, Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim
  • Astros -- Houston Astros, Houston Colt .45's
  • Athletics -- Oakland Athletics, Philadelphia Athletics, Kansas City Athletics, Whatever they are now Athletics
  • Braves -- Boston Braves, Milwaukee Braves, Atlanta Braves
  • Brewers -- Milwaukee Brewers, Seattle Pilots
  • Dodgers -- Los Angeles Dodgers, Brooklyn Dodgers
  • Giants -- San Francisco Giants, New York Giants
  • Guardians -- Cleveland Guardians, Cleveland Indians
  • Marlins -- Miami Marlins, Florida Marlins
  • Nationals -- Washington Nationals, Montreal Expos
  • Orioles -- Baltimore Orioles, St. Louis Browns, 1901 American League Milwaukee Brewers
  • Rangers -- Texas Rangers, Washington Senators between 1961 and 1971
  • Rays -- Tampa Bay Rays, Tampa Bay Devil Rays
  • Twins -- Minnesota Twins, Washington Senators before 1961

The player named Dave Roberts in these lists is a pitcher who played for 8 different teams between 1969 and 1980, not the current manager of the Dodgers.
